Marketing Case Study, May 2026

Best Dress Shoes
for Plantar Fasciitis.

A landing page and two Meta ads built around a new marketing angle for Amberjack. The brief called for launching around the keyword "best dress shoes for plantar fasciitis", an untapped space in their current paid strategy.

01, Strategy

The gap in their current ads.

Going through their ad library, a few ideas immediately came to mind. Wedding occasions are always strong for SEO and perform well in paid social. Tying that into plantar fasciitis felt like a natural move. "Reinventing I Do" is also a direct nod to their own language. They use "Dress Shoes. Reinvented." in their ads, so this puts that same idea into a lifestyle moment. The wedding angle also pulls in more than just the groom: wives and partners shopping for their husbands, mothers of the groom, anyone buying for the wedding party.

What they're running

Checklist-style ads, press badges, broad comfort positioning. "Don't Buy Shoes That Hurt." No occasion or audience-specific creative.

The opportunity

Plantar fasciitis is a high-intent search term with no fashion brand owning it. Occasion targeting (weddings, work, travel) creates moment-based creative with built-in urgency.

Landing page approach

Plantar fasciitis as the primary angle, built on their existing product page format. Wedding, office, and travel positioned as use cases, not the headline.

Ad approach

Two ads, two audiences. One creative departure targeting a high-intent occasion (the groom). One that mirrors their existing format with the new pain point hook.

02, Meta Ads

Two ads, two angles.

Both drive to the landing page. Ad 1 is a new creative direction targeting a high-intent occasion. Ad 2 stays close to Amberjack's existing ad style, making it easy to A/B test against current creative.

03, Landing Page

Built on their existing product page.

The brief suggested copying one of their current product pages as a starting point. The landing page keeps their exact UI patterns: rating row, fit selector, color swatches, size grid, dual CTA buttons, while reframing the copy around plantar fasciitis comfort. Wedding, office, and travel are included as use cases rather than the headline, keeping the SEO angle intact without narrowing the audience. Plantar fasciitis is referenced naturally in one customer review.

04, What I'd Test Next

If this were live.

Three things I'd want to know after launch.

01

Occasion vs. Pain Point

Which ad drives better CPR and conversion: the groom angle or the checklist format? Expect the checklist to win on cold traffic, groom to win on retargeting.

02

Landing Page Headline

Test "Finally. A dress shoe that works with your feet." against a version that leads with the wedding use case. Same page, different entry point.

03

UGC vs. Product Shot

Replace the product image in Ad 2 with a lifestyle shot of someone in a suit, on their feet all day. Amberjack's existing UGC style tends to outperform clean product shots.
